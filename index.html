I'm a grad student at Carnegie Mellon studying electrical and computer engineering, working with Phil Gibbons and Andy Pavlo on problems at the intersection of computer architecture, compiler optimization, and data-intensive systems.

Before CMU, I spent three years at Qualcomm and a year at NVIDIA, both on GPU and SoC architecture teams. I like working at the hardware-software boundary — the layer where you stop abstracting and start measuring what actually happens when bits move through silicon.

Something I Built

LLM inference on mobile GPUs wastes enormous power. The GPU clocks at full speed even when memory bandwidth — not compute — is the bottleneck. I designed a closed-loop system that detects this mismatch and dials back the GPU dynamically, without losing throughput.

US20250356449A1 · Qualcomm Inc · Filed May 2024

A composite metric across memory latency, GPU read bandwidth, and ALU utilization drives clock scaling. A second feedback loop using retired instructions per cycle validates the adjustment — if throughput drops too much, it rolls back.

80% better perf/watt · ~7.5% performance cost
